As per your request, here is a list of questions to ask as you're choosing a physician.

Choosing a physician that meets your needs is a crucial part of getting and staying healthy. A primary care physician can treat many health problems, refer you to a specialist when you need it and help you make important decisions like which screening tests and shots to get.
1) Is he/she covered under your insurance?
Your insurance plan may restrict your choices to a group of plan-approved physicians or offer financial incentives to use plan-affiliated physicians. Always check the terms of your insurance coverage to find out whether the plan will cover visits to the physician you are considering.
 
2) What kind of physician do you need?
A primary care physician can manage your overall care and refer you to specialists when necessary. A specialist focuses on a particular area where you might need care.
 
3) Is the physician board-certified?
Most practicing physicians in the U.S. are board certified. Being board-certified indicates particular expertise in an area such as internal medicine, gynecology, allergy and immunology. This is one way you can be certain of a physician's training.
 
4) Where is the medical office located?
You will want to make sure it is easily accessible, by car or by public transportation.
 
5) Where are routine X-rays and lab tests performed?
Find out if these can be done in-office, or if you will have to go to an outside lab. It’s more convenient if they can be done in the same office and sometimes, outside lab services are not covered by your insurance.
 
6) How long do you have to wait for an appointment after you call?
See if the office's availability meets your needs. Also find out if you can be seen the same day if you have an urgent need.
 
7) Is the office staff friendly and courteous?
Note how helpful and friendly the staff is when you call.
 
8) Does a team manage your care or a single person?
A medical office that offers a personal care team is better able to take care of you. A personal care team is made up of a doctor, nurse, medical assistant and patient service representative. Your team can coordinate your care with specialists and manage long-term chronic illnesses with you.
 
9) Does the medical office use Electronic Health Records?
Electronic Health Records help primary care physicians and specialists coordinate your care, avoid duplicative tests and prevent conflicting medications. It also means that someone who answers the phone at any hour of the day has access to your records.
 
10) Which hospital(s) does the physician use?
If the need arises, your physician may refer you to a specific hospital for treatment. Be sure you are comfortable with the institution he/she refers to.
