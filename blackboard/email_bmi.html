Maintaining a healthy weight may reduce the risk of chronic diseases associated with obesity. To lower or maintain your BMI, here are some important tips to keep in mind:
  • Changing your lifestyle has a higher success rate than short-term fad diets.
  • People who lose weight gradually and steadily
    (1 to 2 pounds per week) are more successful at keeping weight off.
  • To lose weight, you need to use more calories than you take in. As one
    pound equals 3,500 calories, you would need to reduce your caloric
    intake by 500-1000 calories per day to lose about 1 to 2 pounds per week.
  • Even modest weight loss (5 to 10 percent of your total body weight)
    is likely to produce health benefits.
